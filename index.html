🧶 Knot Guide
🧵 Palomar Knot Most Reliable
  1. Double 6–10 inches of line and pass it through the hook eye
  2. Tie a simple overhand knot with the doubled line (don’t tighten yet)
  3. Pass the loop over the entire hook or lure
  4. Wet with saliva and pull both the tag end and standing line to tighten
  5. Trim the tag end close
✅ Best for: Hooks, jigs, Texas rigs, drop shots — braided and fluorocarbon
🧵 Improved Clinch Quick & Easy
  1. Thread 6 inches of line through the hook eye
  2. Wrap the tag end around the standing line 5–7 times
  3. Pass the tag end through the small loop near the eye
  4. Then pass it back through the big loop you just created
  5. Wet and pull tight against the eye, trim tag
✅ Best for: Crankbaits, spinnerbaits, topwater — mono and fluorocarbon up to 20lb
🧵 Uni Knot Fluorocarbon King
  1. Run 6 inches of line through the eye and fold back parallel
  2. Make a loop by laying the tag end over the doubled line
  3. Wrap the tag end through the loop and around both lines 5–6 times
  4. Wet and pull the tag end to tighten the coils
  5. Slide the knot down to the eye by pulling the standing line
✅ Best for: All-purpose, especially fluorocarbon. Also used as a line-to-line connection (double uni)
🧵 Snell Knot Flipping Power
  1. Thread line through the hook eye toward the point, leave 8 inches of tag
  2. Make a loop below the hook shank
  3. Wrap the tag end around the shank and through the loop 6–7 times, winding toward the eye
  4. Hold wraps in place, pull the tag end tight
  5. Then pull standing line to seat the knot at the eye
✅ Best for: Flipping jigs, Texas rigs, EWG hooks — keeps the hook point up for better hooksets
🧵 Loop Knot (Rapala) More Action
  1. Tie an overhand knot 4 inches from the end, don’t tighten
  2. Pass the tag end through the lure eye
  3. Bring tag end back through the overhand knot (same side you exited)
  4. Wrap the tag end around the standing line 3 times
  5. Pass tag end back through the overhand knot, wet and tighten
✅ Best for: Jerkbaits, topwater, swimbaits — the loop gives the lure more freedom to move
💡 Pro Tips
Water temp is the #1 factor in bass behavior. Below 50°F, slow everything down — long pauses, drag-and-deadstick presentations. Above 70°F, speed up and cover water.
Fish the first and last hour of light. Bass feed most aggressively during low-light transitions regardless of season. Full and new moon phases create even stronger feeding windows.
Wind is your friend. Wind pushes baitfish into banks and points, activates bass into feeding mode, and breaks up your line’s visibility. Fish the windblown bank first.
Match your line to water clarity. Clear = 6–10lb fluoro, stained = 12–15lb fluoro, muddy = 40–65lb braid. Lighter line in clear water means more bites.
When you catch a fish, don’t leave. Make 10+ more casts to the same area. Bass often school up, and the first bite usually means more are nearby.
